Frequently Asked Questions

  • 01 How can I request CAG to promote my work?

    All you have to do is call us on the numbers mentioned on our website or send an email to info@cogitoartgallery.com and a member of the Operations Team will get in touch with you within 48-72 hours.

  • 02 For how low long will the gallery be representing you or your art?

    The standard term of the contract is six months to a year, after which time the contract can either be renegotiated, or if both parties are in total agreement, language in the contract will allow for the term to be automatically renewed.

  • 03 What local, regional, national or international regions will the representation cover?

    We will actively promote all your works but we will promote the artworks consigned to us more aggressively. You are free to promote your own works, both online and offline, but you cannot enter into a transaction for a work that is available for purchase on our website and/ or has been consigned to us for a particular amount of time.

  • 04 Will you be allowed to sell any art online?

    We will start off by promoting your art at the local/ district/ city level. In time, we will gradually revise or expand the geographic parameters of the agreement.

  • 05 Will the gallery be representing all of your art or specific works or bodies of work?

    The gallery will have exclusive rights to sell only the works or body of work that are in the gallery's catalogue and/ or the website. However, in exhibitions and featured events, we will promote all your sold/ unsold and/ or past/ present works to the extent it is feasible to do so.

  • 06 Will specific works of art be consigned to the gallery?

    es. You will be required to consign the works designated for promotions and/ or auctions and/ or exhibitions with us. These works will be accurately and fully itemized including descriptions, dimensions, medium and any other relevant details, and all this along with images of each work. The time period during which your art will be in possession of the gallery will also be specified, after which you would be free to take any or all works back. This time period can always be renegotiated or automatically renewed in writing.

  • 07Will you be able to sell art on your own outside of the gallery?

    Yes. You can continue selling your works on your website, at your studio, at other galleries, or on social media pages. CAG will not be entitled to any of the proceeds of works that have NOT been consigned to and/ or are sold through CAG.

  • 08 Who will determine the look and layout of the exhibition in which your works will be showcased?

    CAG will do the needful in this regard. We will look forward to your inputs but the final decision rests with CAG's management.

  • 09If expenses are involved in promotions, shows or exhibitions, who will be responsible and for what percentages of the total cost?

    CAG will bear all expenses in this regard.

  • 10How will prices be determined?

    In most cases, the gallery will discuss the base price with you and then set retail prices ahead of time. Base price is nothing but the minimum price for which a particular artwork can be sold. Retail price is the price at which the work will be offered to clients. The latter will be decided by the gallery at its sole discretion.

  • 11Does the gallery have the ability to reduce a retail price if a potential buyer makes an offer? If yes, what percentage can that price be reduced?

    Yes. Unless otherwise agreed upon in writing, a negotiating range of ten (10%) to thirty (30%) per cent on the retail price is common practice.

  • 12How will the gallery keep records of sales, and should there be a question involving whether specific artworks have actually sold, how will you be able to verify that those sales have in fact taken place?

    The artist is within his rights to ask for a report of sales activity solely for his works once every quarter. However, the gallery may use its own discretion to decide the date of furnishing such information.

  • 13Who pays incidental expenses like transport, packing, shipping, storage, travel, and so on?

    Yes. Unless otherwise agreed upon in writing, a negotiating range of ten (10%) to thirty (30%) per cent on the retail price is common practice.

  • 14Who pays for framing, restoration or similar expenses involved in preparing the work(s) for sale, show and/ or exhibitions?

    Any expenses incurred in framing, restoration or similar expenses will be shared equally by the artist and gallery. The artist's share of the expenses will be deducted from the base price of the work.
