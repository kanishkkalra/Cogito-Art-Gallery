Swapan Sutradhar

Swapan Sutradhar’s paintings mostly portray the recollections from his memories since childhood, and his personal observations and experiences. Forms, colors and textures are at once aids to expressing his thoughts, emotions and subjects, and key constituent elements of his works. Distorted human figures, portraits and other assorted objects are also important components of the integrative pictorial expression of his subjects. His recent works are at once illustrative and symbolic: they are an eclectic, recurrent combination of as disparate motifs as sun, moon, king, queen, man, woman and natural phenomena. The relief arrangements of these motifs play a significant role in conveying the subject. The palette is simple and minimal, dominated by a few colors, but is effective in creating a powerful imagery. The layered application of different kinds of materials, including waste, also heightens the impact of his works.
