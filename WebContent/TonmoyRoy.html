Tonmoy Roy

Tonmoy Roy draws on images and sights from situations of everyday life, and his own experiences and memories to create appealing visual narratives on themes as diverse as the blissful dream-world of a child and the worrisome state of global violence. Bold use of bright colors with fewer muted tones heighten the dramatic effect of his compositions, while eclectic images, whether of human beings, animals, birds, mythical beings, plants, trees, hills, houses, and water bodies, or geometric patterns and abstract designs, are so arranged as to weave together a layered narrative charged with energy and suffused with emotions and meanings. Human experience of life and world are central to his works that blend the microcosm of inner feelings, memories, aspirations, and yearnings with the macrocosm of worldly experiences and realities. Further, his works abound in the deft use of ‘beings and things’ as allegories of abstract ideas and personal views.
